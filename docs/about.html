What's your story?

I've spent 30+ years on both sides of the camera. As a TV reporter, I interviewed execs and professionals, athletes and rock stars, politicians and plain old people.

I've dedicated my career to helping people communicate their unique stories. I believe the stories that make the greatest impact are honest and credible, engaging and entertaining.

Fun fact: Dolly Parton and I enjoyed sweet tea together after our interview in Dollywood, TN.

image of logos

Master Interviewer

I've interviewed scientists, exploreres and environmentalists for National Geographic. I interviewed influencers for Oprah's 2020 National Tour.

For home makeover shows on HGTV and DIY, I've interviewed tv stars and regular folks looking for 15 minutes of fame and a new bathroom. I interviewed interesting people in exotic locations for The Travel Channel. My work for Animal Planet included interviewing dog owner and their dogs. The dogs were way cooler.

When I was interviewing on the campaign trail, Jesse Ventura taught me a sweet thumb wrestling move. He was a pro wrestler. And then Minnesota's governor.

Experienced Media Trainer

I've media coached high-profile lawyers and Mayo Clinic doctors. I'll admit, all the doctors I coached were super intelligent, but they definitely needed guidance on refining their messages and keeping it brief.

I have a master's degree in broadcast journalism from Northwestern University's Medill School of Journalism. My master's program included working in Washington, DC where I covered stories at the White House and the Capitol.

I have a BA in Speech/Communications from Boston College.

I live in the Twin Cities. You might find me walking around one of the lakes. Or working on my latest project: hand-painting cool signs on century-old reclaimed barnwood. See more at www.reclaimedwords.com

What does this all mean for you?

My vast experience can help you identify and illuminate your story so you can be prepared to shine in your next interview.
